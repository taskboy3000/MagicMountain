# Faction Editorial Style Guide

For more detailed explanations of factions, see "The Factions of Magic Mountain.md".

## Core Principle

Each faction should have a recognizable voice that reflects its worldview.  No faction believes they are extreme or illogical.  No customer from a faction is a mustache-twirling villian or paladin in shining plate mail.  Each is a real human with a believe system valuable to them.

Some members of factions will absolutely think they are the Main Character of the story. None of them are right.

The player should eventually identify a faction from its vocabulary and tone alone, even if the faction name is hidden.

Every line of dialogue should answer one question:

> **"What does this faction believe the Mountain is for?"**

The factions should never sound like palette-swapped NPCs. They are competing philosophies wearing human faces.

# The Syndicate

### Core Belief

The Syndicate believes commerce organizes society.  Everything has a price.  The Mountain is a resource.  A wasted opportunity is immoral.

The Syndicate seek to assuage emotional distemper with temporal prosperity, but this fails to sate their spiritual longing.

### Negotiation Style

* Professional & Efficient
* Direct, but friendly only when profitable
* Always calculating the value of people and things

### Emotional Temperature

Warm enough to close a deal.  Never warm enough to become friends.

### Vocabulary

investment

inventory

margin

asset

client

shipment

contract

quality

market

return

opportunity

### Avoid

Mysticism

Scientific jargon

Moral arguments

Grand speeches

### Typical Attitude

"If this involves money, I'm in."

# LibreMount

### Core Belief

LibreMount believes power should be decentralized.  The Mountain belongs to everyone.  Entrentched forces want to use the Mountain to enslave us.  Gatekeeping technology is immoral.

LibreMount value equality, liberty, and fraternity.  And if you don't believe this too, they have guns.

### Negotiation Style

* Respectful
* Cooperative
* Community-orient
* Honest
* Militant
* Distrustful of commerce

### Emotional Temperature

Folksy

Community-oriented

Guerilla fighters

### Vocabulary

community

repair

community

camp

share

neighbors

survive

restore

useful

needed

### Avoid

Greed

Religious certainty

Cold bureaucracy

Fascism

### Typical Attitude

"A working water purifier is worth more than an upcycled weapon."

# The Faculty

### Core Belief

The Faculty believes increasing the store of human knowledge is a moral obligation.  Understanding is caring.  Knowledge is power.  Ignorance is slavery.

The Faculty prize reason in order to rebuild civilization, but they lack empathy for the people around them.

### Negotiation Style

* Curious
* Analytical
* Distracted by interesting discoveries
* Often forgets social niceties

### Emotional Temperature

Detached.

Intellectually excited.

Personally reserved.

Somewhat short-tempered.

Elitist.

### Vocabulary

specimen

analysis

classification

hypothesis

correlation

sample

anomaly

field notes

observation

evidence

### Avoid

Emotional language

Religious language

Commercial language

Military language

### Typical Attitude

"This artifact is far too valuable for rank commerce."

# The Purifiers

### Core Belief

The Purifiers believe that old ideas and inventions are dangerous and must be strictly controlled. The Mountain is old, unpredicable, and dangerous.

Curiosity caused the old world's collapse.  Isolate the danger of new ideas before it infects us all.

The Purifiers seej protect people by putting them in cages.

### Negotiation Style

* Stern
* Suspicious
* Judgmental
* Quick to reject
* Slow to trust

### Emotional Temperature

Holds grudges.

Righteous anger.

Persecuted.

### Vocabulary

cleanse

quarantine

corruption

hazard

faith 

discipline

sacrifice

duty

moral hazzard

### Avoid

Wonder

Excitement

Humor

Academic speculation

Intellect

### Typical Attitude

"The old world was judged and found wanting."

# The Revelationists

### Core Belief

The Revelationists believe the mountain is the universe manifesting itself.  We are the cosmos.  Artifacts are the message, not the medium.  Coincidence is narration.

The Revelationists see evidence all around them that the universe is alive, but they miss people in front of them.

### Negotiation Style

* Calm
* Off-beat
* Polite
* Speaks in strange metaphors
* Rarely answers questions directly

### Emotional Temperature

Serene.

Quietly convinced.

Never frantic.

Luna Lovegood.

### Vocabulary

sign

calling

pattern

pilgrimage

awakening

witness

listen

gift

veil

truth

### Avoid

Engineering language

Economics/Trade

Scientific certainty

Urgency

### Typical Attitude

"The message of the mountain awaits us all.  You need but listen."

# Shared Editorial Rules

Every faction should interpret the same event differently.

A valuable artifact is never just valuable.

It is:

* profit to the Syndicate
* communal property to LibreMount
* the expansion of human knowledge to the Faculty
* a scacred duty to contain for the Purifiers
* a message to the Revelationists

---

# Market Behavior

Each faction should negotiate according to its philosophy.

**Syndicate**

Negotiation is business.  Business is life.

**LibreMount**

Negotiation is a regretable necessity.  The community needs equipment.  Community matters. 

**Faculty**

The expansion of human knowledge is a moral obligation.  Novel artifacts broaden intellectual inquiry.

**Purifiers**

Every artifact is inherently evil.  Confiscating evil is our highest good.

**Revelationists**

The mountain is the universe revealing itself to us.  What price can one put on cosmic intercourse?

# Emotional Arc

The player should gradually develop emotional expectations.

Syndicate arrives:

> "This rube does not know whom they are negotiating with"

LibreMount arrives:

> "People without means will die without this"

Faculty arrives:

> "This uncouth peddler has no clue what treasure they possess"

Purifiers arrive:

> "The taint of the old world must be contained"

Revelationists arrive:

> "Dude, dig that that artifact's vibe!"

Those reactions are the goal.

---

# Final Editorial Test

A faction is never lying to the player.

Each faction sincerely believes its own worldview.

They may be biased.

They may be mistaken.

They may be manipulative.

But they are never written as cartoon villains.

Every faction believes it is trying to save humanity.

Before writing faction dialogue, ask:

1. Does this line reveal the faction's worldview?

2. Could another faction plausibly have said it?

If the answer to #2 is yes, rewrite it until only one faction could have spoken those words.
